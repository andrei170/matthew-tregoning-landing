FAQ

Common questions, before opting in.

What does "$0 down" actually mean?

It means you don't buy the unit. You sign a long-term lease with the property owner, structure it as a short-term rental sub-lease (with the right legal framework), and you operate it as the host. You're paying rent to the owner. The owner gets a guaranteed tenant. You get the AirBnB upside. The framework walks the legal structure, the owner conversation, and the operational math.

Is this rental arbitrage or AirBnB hosting?

It's rental arbitrage as the starting wedge. Most operators in my cohort start with arbitrage units, then layer in owned units once the operating cash flow supports the down-payment math. The framework covers both - you'll learn which one runs the better numbers in your specific market.

Do I really not need capital?

You need some capital. The lease deposit, the initial furniture cost, the cleaning fees, the channel manager subscription. The "$0 down" refers to the property acquisition - you don't need a down payment on a property because you're not buying one. The capital required to start an arbitrage unit is typically in the low five-figures. The framework walks the honest budget.

"First Unit In 90 Days." Ten to fourteen modules. Owner-pitch scripts, market vetting checklists, legal-structure templates, the operational system. Sits at a sub-$500 lifetime tier. Designed for the operator who wants the framework recorded and runs it solo.
